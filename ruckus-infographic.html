User Interface: Ruckus Immersive Infographic

January 2019 – April 2019

Overview

Background

Government Executive has a research department, Government Business Council (GBC), that surveys government officials about varying themes. Clients pay GBC for the ability to curate the topics of the research. In spring of 2019, Ruckus sponsored GBC research of government officials’ understanding of the recently released Citizens Broadband Radio Service.

Challenge

Usually GBC delivers their results in the form of a survey report, but in this case in addition to the report, we produced an immersive infographic. Considering this was new territory, we were figuring out process and platform capabilities as we go.

My Role

I worked with GBC researchers to organize the results from the survey into a story and lay it out digitally.

Discover

Review survey results & branding

The launch of the infographic was tied to the SXSW 2019 event. However, the information was dependent on finishing the survey. We ended up having 2 weeks between publishing the survey report and the Infographic. To aid the time crunch, I found the Ruckus branding guidelines and asked for the research information pre-publishment.

tl;dr
I spent a week familiarizing myself with the branding guideline and doing some independent research. I dedicated another week to reviewing the survey results.

Synthesize

Written Content

The next step was to synthesize results and independent research into a story. I went back and forth revising the content with the researcher in a Google Doc removing, adding, and editing information until we were both satisfied. His focus was on accuracy and portrayal, mine was on engagement and incorporating visuals.

tl;dr
I worked with the researcher to synthesize the information in a Google Doc before wireframing it.

Ideate

Wireframe

At this point, the researcher and I had communicated with each other and had a mutual understanding of what the site would look like. However, we needed something to show the client to get them on board as well. I made a mock up of the site using black and white stick figure visuals, but all the correct copy.

tl;dr
We needed to get client buy-in with a wireframe of the site.

Deliverable

Immersive Infographic

The final product was built in a stand-alone Wordpress site using Elementor. I pulled starting points for the illustrations off of iStock and added plugins to get the capabilities like card-flips and slide in effects. Ultimately, the client was really happy with the site and wanted us to create a PDF version for them to pass out at future conferences!

tl;dr
Each event got their own digital ads and physical day-of signage.
