Leadership Voices

About

Government Executive’s “Leadership Voices” channel is a place to showcase top SMEs in the government consulting world. The pages could either be video format or article format, depending on the client needs. As a two year old product, it has yet to be integrated into the site's CMS and was split across two different drag-and-drop platforms (Wordpress and Shorthand). If the page was to be a video, it was built in Wordpress and the URL was masked by our IT department to look like a page internal to the CMS. If the page was an article it was built in Shorthand, downloaded as raw code, and uploaded directly into our CMS; this process prevented editing post-publish.

My Role

The first step to saving the company money and streamlining the infrastructure of these pages was researching opportunities to replace shorthand. I eventually found that I could build all internal pages within wordpress, and worked with Devs to transfer the build of the landing page to the CMS. I used Elementor, a free wordpress plug-in, to make it possible for all team members to build pages. Inside elementor I could make templates that made the page as easy as swapping out images and text. Finally, I capitalized on my other skills and took on editing the videos and photographing SMEs to use for ad suites.

Challenge

Due to previous negative history with Wordpress, generating buy-in was difficult. I had to find something scalable - useable by non-technical employees - and free. Our CMS was built to cater to reporters for the editorial side of business. Building the capabilities to rival a third-party would take extensive Dev hours that were not available. Because our landing page and video pages were already built in Wordpress, and Elementor was a free plug-in that allowed the capabilities we needed, I eventually garnered this buy-in and was able to work solely out of Wordpress.

Deliverables

  • Migration support to Wordpress
  • Photoshoots with Various SMEs
  • Video Editing
  • Individual Page Creation
  • Promotional Ad Suites

Insights

Change is difficult, especially when it requires high-level buy-in. Doing your research and sticking to what works provides the best bet. Using third-party providers can be an investment, but is a better route to experimenting and figuring out what works. These pages now can be standardizes and moved into the dev-supported CMS. Finally, skills you’ve learned in a previously life can be transferable - whether that is knowing your way around a camera or knowing your way around a car jack.
