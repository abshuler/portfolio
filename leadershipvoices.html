GovExec Leadership Voices. A better internal UX.

Overview

Government Executive, a government facing magazine, sells promotional packages to marketers to showcase their thought leadership in the government space in the hopes of winning government agencies’ contracts. Leadership Voices can be a video or article page centered around a SME, subject matter expert, from their company.

Research

Internally, the company uses a content management system (CMS) handled by a team of developers. Due to limited development resources, the company had originally decided to use a third party platform, Wordpress, to build a landing page that was masked to look as if it was a part of the CMS.

Because previous designers were unfamiliar with Wordpress, they decided to use another third party provided, Shorthand, to build the individual article pages. These pages were zipped and downloaded as hard code, uploaded into the CMS, and then linked from the home page that was built out in Wordpress. The process was confusing and convoluted, to say the least.

Requirements

The process needed to be simplified. Wordpress is capable of becoming a ‘plug and play no code’ environment with the help of a plug in called Elementor. This meant the article pages, video pages, and landing page could all be built in one platform and masked to look as if it was part of the same website as the CMS.

Framework

After making and selling the use case, developing the framework was easy. Elementor allows for the creation of ‘template’ pages that can be reused without limit. Wordpress Parent Pages allowed the URL to be customized to the client sponsoring the program, and additionally, the pages were mobile responsive.
