GovExec Leadership Voices. A better internal UX.

Overview

Government Executive, a government facing magazine, sells promotional packages to marketers to showcase their thought leadership in the government space in the hopes of winning government agencies’ contracts. Leadership Voices can be a video or article page centered around a SME, subject matter expert, from their company.

Research

Internally, the company uses a content management system (CMS) handled by a team of developers. Due to limited development resources, the company had originally decided to use a third party platform, Wordpress, to build a landing page that was masked to look as if it was a part of the CMS. Because previous designers were unfamiliar with Wordpress, they decided to use another third party provided, Shorthand, to build the individual article pages. These pages were zipped and downloaded as hard code, uploaded into the CMS, and then linked from the home page that was built out in Wordpress. The process was confusing and convoluted, to say the least.

Requirements

The process needed to be simplified. Wordpress is capable of becoming a ‘plug and play no code’ environment with the help of a plug in called Elementor. This meant the article pages, video pages, and landing page could all be built in one platform and masked to look as if it was part of the same website as the CMS.

Framework

After making and selling the use case, developing the framework was easy. Elementor allows for the creation of ‘template’ pages that can be reused without limit. Wordpress Parent Pages allowed the URL to be customized to the client sponsoring the program, and additionally, the pages were mobile responsive.

Additional Work

This grew out of a personal need I saw in the business unit. As the sole designer on a team of four handling a million dollars of contract work, I needed a more efficient way to stand up these pages to keep up with demand. Along with cutting out the cost of using Shorthand, I also cut the cost of hiring a freelance photographer to photo the SME. I instead drew from my education in photography at USC and a camera previously owned by the company to create ¾ headshots we could use for the site. For our video work, I integrated stock video, two camera angles, text overlays, and after effects graphics to create more visual interest from our mostly talking head videos. Our watch time increased from :43 to 2:13 over the course of a year.
