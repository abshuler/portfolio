Website Rebuild: Global CI

January 2020 – ongoing

Overview

Background

Through my network I connected to Global CI, an IT consultancy, who felt their current website portrayed their company as a “staffing agency” and were looking for a revamp. They also had recently launched a new logo for their company and wanted to refresh their branding to match.

Challenge

Global CI struggled to communicate their offerings to their customers through content, website layout, and downloadable assets. This meant their website was drawing minimum traffic and not being utilitized as a business generator. Without a dedicated UX team (or web manager, product owner, etc), they needed outside help to accomplish this.

My Role

I am (still) working to synthesize the CEO’s ideas of his company into a viable prototype, test content engagement with users, and refine the website based on feedback.

Discover

Current Website Audit

Looking beyond the inconsistencies in the user interface (varying type faces, display issues on smaller browser sizes, spacing, etc), I wanted to know how users were currently finding the site, how they were interacting with it, and how the content is set up. What I found was that most searches were direct from a desktop, and nearly 2/3 of users left immediately. There were three main pages and the 8 tabs of high level navigation linked to anchors among the three pages.

Main Takeaways

Interviewees said they had limited time and money to dedicate to breaking into or learning more about the field.

They felt there were too many vocabulary terms and resources available to them.

They liked being able to quickly scan what they were looking for, whether it was a video, article, or topic.

1. limited time and money

2. too much available

3. quickly scan for information

I conducted interviews with UX professionals new to the field to see how they were currently learning about the industry.

Synthesize Phase One

Affinity Mapping

Once I had conducted enough interviews, I mapped their responses on post-it notes and organized them to analyze themes. I found some people preferred videos and some written content, but all agreed they wanted to consume the content quickly whether via skimming (written) or multitasking (audio).

tldr;
Affinity mapping allowed me to analyze and organize the feedback from my interviews.

Synthesize Phase Two

Feature Prioritization

While some of the competition was more advanced with custom animations, ad placements, and a daily send, I needed to determine what was necessary and easy in order to launch. Afterward, I could iterate on features.

tldr;
Essentials features: reoccurring content, links to external content (to accomodate video watchers), accessibility, and ‘unsubscribe’ option.

Deliverable

High Fidelity Prototype

Again using Figma, I created a higher fidelity prototype. I also created a mock up of the content of a newsletter and included an example on the site of the newsletter you would get.

Usability Testing

A lot of this usability testing involved feedback on the copy. I had user objectives such as “explain to me what the product is” or “find the example” and because things were not written out in plain English they were hard to understand. I also found out users would not navigate to the second section on the home page if there was no hinting to it, such as a down arrow or small visibility from the home.

Next Steps

Develop

Because I didn’t chose to create an app, this is a project I can actually start to work on. My plan is to develop a landing page in bootstrap (similar to this site) and gather a following.

User Interviews

To continue improving the product I will need to continously interview my users. Thankfully, I have some UX connections to help with this. I also plan to include a short survey about which part of the newsletter was the user’s favorite.

KPI Indicators

Another important indicator of success will be engagement-related KPIs. I can do A/B testing with headlines, look at open rates, test best time of days to send, and experiment with improving sign up numbers.

tldr;
Next steps will be to set this newsletter live. I will continue to interview users, look at KPIs and use some A/B testing to improve the product.
