Portraits

About

A series of headshots for Subject Matter Expert interviews. Each photo accompanies an article showcased in Government Executive’s “Leadership Voices” channel.

My Role

Originally, my job was just to design the pages of the articles, but I eventually took on the photography as well. I quickly learned I had to put my subject at ease in order to get the best shot. I traveled to client locations to take photos, shared photos with the client for review, and then edited those that ended up on the site.

Challenge

Government Executive was paying a contractor to come in and take photos just for us to use one or two in our final product. I saw a need for an in-house photographer, and having been trained, volunteered for the job. We had an in-house camera with a very specific lens - a 50mm prime - that was hard to shoot with as subjects were already uncomfortable and I could not get close. I advocated for a new lens, in the meantime used my personal camera, and managed to capture six Subject Matter Experts for the pieces - once changing a tire to insure we arrived on time.

Insights

Skills you’ve learned in a previously life can be transferable - whether that is knowing your way around a camera or knowing your way around a car jack.
