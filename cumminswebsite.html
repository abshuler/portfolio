Cummins Fire Power Drive Engine.
Driving their website into the 21st century.

Overview

Cummins, Inc. is a worldwide engine manufacturer that produces anything from home generators to fire truck engines. Before announcing their acquisition of Cummins Power Generation in 2017, they wanted to integrate their stand alone sites for smaller products, such as the Fire Pump Drive Engine.

Research

Working as a 30 day contractor I had little time to lose in conducting stakeholder and user interviews. Fire Pump Engines power the emergency water supply in tall buildings and are rarely replaced, with the engines dating to the 1980s still being serviced by Cummins. This site was to be used as a sales tool for new engines, a resource hub for maintenance on in-use engines, and a registration portal for engineers wishing to learn to install these engines. I conducted qualitative interviews with engineers, technical writers, sales representatives, maintenance support representatives, and installation trainers.

Modeling

Two primary personas emerged early on - those wishing to buy and sell models, and those wishing to maintenance and service models. These personas covered both internal and external users, as their use environments and motivations were nearly identical. Installation engineers became a secondary persona as they would only use the website to sign up for a training session.

Requirements

Engines require a lot of technical information in order to be bought. Selling personas need to know their compatibility with country regulations on emissions, additional necessary hardware, power ratings, compatibility with pumps, price quotes, and other marketing information that sets these engines apart. Additionally, to service an engine a user might need wiring diagrams, installation drawings, manuals, part numbers, and warranty information. The client’s original plan was to mix these two personas together for each of the 11 engines meaning each engine could have 11 documents linked out, with no discernation if it was sales materials or service materials. Instead, I created a separate drop down for Maintenance and Warranty allowing peronas to self segregate up front and simplify the information they received.

Framework

The Cummins De Pere, WI location is the only manufacturer to sell Fire Pump Drive Engines and employes no one with technical design experience. I needed a tool that was going to be able to show design elements in a low-tech way that would be easy to understand if executives needed to make changes when my contract ended. I chose to build out the mock ups in Excel, changing cells’ width and color to mimic what the page would look like and creating internal links to show user flow.

Additional Work

Since the engines required printable content such as spec sheets, I worked to redesign the print-outs in a new template.
