Cummins, Inc. Website

About

Cummins, Inc. is a worldwide engine manufacturer that produces anything from home generators to fire truck engines. Specifically, this page talks about the promotion and website for their fire pump drive engines - a specific engine that powers a water pump for emergency sprinkler systems in high-rise buildings. As Cummins corporate bought back their franchises, they worked to integrate specialty products’ sites into their larger brand site - cummins.com.

My Role

As a short-term contractor I was tasked with taking the content from their small, self-supported site last serviced circa 2005, and reorganizing it into the larger cummins site. That involved: talking to engineers, salespeople, and installation trainers to figure out pertinent information; fabricating a marketing angle from unique features; taking photos to populate the site of both engines and marketing collateral; and building a mock-up in software they could edit at the end of the contract.

Challenge

Cummins’ fire pump drive engines almost entirely are produced in their De Pere, WI location - an office who’s marketing department lives in the regional office 6 hours away. The De Pere office is populated with engineers, warehouse manufacturers, and maintenance support. In this position, I had to learn all of the technical information before I knew how to organize it and address the variety of needs. The content had to be a hub for those looking to buy, sell, install, and maintain engines.

Deliverables

  • Redesigned specification sheets
  • A wireframe built in Excel
  • Technical language and photos to go on the website

Insights

Designers are problem solvers that often need to adapt to the needs of their clients. In this case, I had to find software that could be edited by non-designers and worked within what the employees already had access to. I decided to use a software most office folks are familiar with that could provide me the most design freedom - Excel. Excel was easy to use, allowed me to mock several pages in the same document, link between pages, and allowed some design touches, as displayed right.
