Who is Amelia Shuler?

I am not sure I am supposed to have an answer for that at age 23, let alone at any age. The one thing I do know is I have always been amazed by sunflowers. Corny, I know but stay with me here - sunflowers are inherently connotated with the dainty, feminine ideal of flowers, but they rebel. The are rigid, and can grow to ten feet tall. No human is going to accidentally step and kill a sunflower: they're resilient. Sunflowers are versitale, they can answer the call of an empty stomach or answer the question 'does he love me, does he love me not?' They're useful and invasive, they're strong and fragile, can be feminine or grungy. Sunflowers are a compliation of contradictions, and they remind me a lot of myself. In trying to create this portfolio site I tried to determine what I wanted to portray about myself. Sunflowers inspired me; in my color scheme, my font pairing, and the ideaology I hold. Think outside of the norm, but be able to fit the mold when necessary.

Just like a sunflower.
