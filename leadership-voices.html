Internal Process and Product Development: Leadership Voices

September 2018 – february 2019

Overview

Background

Government Executive, a government facing magazine, sells promotional packages to marketers to showcase their thought leadership in the government space in the hopes of winning government agencies’ contracts. Leadership Voices can be a video or article page centered around a subject matter expert (SME) from their company. Leadership Voices was at the time built between two third-party providers, Shorthand and Wordpress and linked to the internal Content Management System (CMS).

Challenge

During a downscaling initiative, Leadership Voices’ production process was under review for the time and specialty it took, along with the associated cost. In short, Government Executive needed a simpler, cheaper way to scale this product to all team members.

My Role

At the time, my manager asked me to help her search for other options. It had to look as if it was part of the CMS, allow for dynamic pages, work in a drag-and-drop environment, and not require coding knowledge.

Discover

Competitive Analysis

Step 1 was clear – what options were available to us and at what cost? I also made sure to ask stakeholders (my manager and her manager) what the main concerns were and discovered cost and maintainence were most important to them. I created a competitive analysis of a few systems out there.

tldr;
I compared options taking special care to the two factors imporant to stakeholders: management and cost.

Synthesize

User Flow

Previously, if a user wanted to create an article Leadership Voices page, they would build the design of the page in Shorthand. Once it was approved by the client, the user zipped and downloaded the files as hard code and uploaded them into our CMS. Finally, they would add a tile to the WordPress landing page (built using Elementor) to indicate the page was “live.” The key points were users needed to create a page, have it approved, and set it “live” in the internal CMS.

tldr;
The current user flow was complicated with few key steps: make the page, get approval, and set it “live” on the internal CMS.

Ideate & Validate

High-fidelity mockup

I created templates based off the previous Shorthand pages in Elementor using Wordpress after getting approval from my manager. Knowing our stakeholders, historically they responded better to having something handed to them in the most refined version possible, rather than including them in the research.

Stakeholder buy-in

We presented our findings from the research and our plan for maintainence and implementation. We also showed the extensive Wordpress and Elementor support libraries, the templates we made, and answered any further questions. However, after buy-in we also needed to make sure it worked for the people buying these products, our clients.

tldr;
We created a high-fidelity mockup to convince our internal stakeholders.

Refinement

Client Feedback

After working with our clients, we ended up having to add a few capabilities to the site. First off, never in our history had this product been “mobile-responsive,” however this is something our clients had continually asked about. I iterated over time to remake the templates to allow them to be mobile responsive along with desktop compatible.
Occassionally, we had two clients sponsor one article/video. With the logo in the top right, would each logo be able to link to each clients’ landing page? This meant adjusting the template again to adjust the grid for two logos.

tldr;
As clients bought more Leadership Voices, we refined the templates to fit their needs.
